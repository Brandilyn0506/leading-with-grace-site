Leadership Talks / Webinars

Curated talks and webinars to spark reflection, deepen insight, and support real-world leadership.

The Leadership Talks / Webinars section brings together video teachings and key ideas from respected voices in leadership, vulnerability, culture, and everyday influence. These talks are ideal for personal reflection, lunch-and-learns, or small group discussion. Over time, this page can also host your own recorded sessions, transcripts, and downloadable notes.

Overview

Each featured talk includes a brief summary, why it matters for leaders, and prompts you can use to debrief on your own or with a team. Whenever possible, links point to official sources (such as TED.com) where transcripts and subtitles are available.

You can use these talks to:

  • Introduce a leadership concept at the start of a meeting or training.
  • Kick off a discussion about culture, trust, or communication.
  • Reflect on your own habits, assumptions, and growth areas as a leader.
  • Provide shared language and examples for new managers.

Featured Talks

How Great Leaders Inspire Action – Simon Sinek

Topic: Purpose & Vision · Format: TED Talk

Simon Sinek introduces the idea of starting with “why”—the deeper purpose or belief behind what you do. He explains the “Golden Circle” (Why–How–What) and shows how great leaders inspire others by communicating from the inside out.

Why it matters: New leaders often focus on tasks and expectations. This talk reminds you that people are more engaged when they understand the purpose behind the work.

Reflect: How clearly can you explain the “why” behind your team’s work in one or two sentences?

The Power of Vulnerability – Brené Brown

Topic: Courage, Connection & Authenticity · Format: TED Talk

Brené Brown explores how vulnerability is not weakness, but a path to connection, courage, and growth. She shares research and stories that challenge the idea that leaders must always appear strong and certain.

Why it matters: Leaders who pretend to “have it all together” can unintentionally create distance. Appropriate vulnerability makes it safer for others to speak up, learn, and try new things.

Reflect: Where might you need to move from “performing” to being honest about what you’re learning?

Watch Talk & View Transcript

Everyday Leadership – Drew Dudley

Topic: Everyday Influence · Format: TED Talk

Drew Dudley reframes leadership as the small, meaningful moments where we positively affect others—often without realizing it. He challenges the idea that leadership is reserved for a few people with titles.

Why it matters: New managers can feel pressure to “be a leader” in big ways and miss the power of daily interactions. This talk reinforces that leadership is built in ordinary moments.

Reflect: Who has led you in a small but meaningful way—and have you ever told them?

Watch Talk & View Transcript

What It Takes to Be a Great Leader – Roselinde Torres

Topic: Modern Leadership · Format: TED Talk

Roselinde Torres shares insights from years of observing leaders around the world and asks three key questions that distinguish leaders who are ready for the future from those who are repeating the past.

Why it matters: The world of work is changing quickly. Leaders who adapt, seek diverse input, and look ahead are better prepared to serve their teams well.

Reflect: How often do you intentionally place yourself in situations where your worldview is stretched?

Watch Talk & View Transcript

Lead Like the Great Conductors – Itay Talgam

Topic: Leadership Style & Influence · Format: TED Talk

Itay Talgam uses orchestra conductors as a picture of different leadership styles—controlling, hands-off, collaborative—and shows how great leaders create conditions where others can contribute their best.

Why it matters: Leaders don’t just “play all the instruments”—they bring out the best in others. This talk offers a memorable visual for how your style affects the people you lead.

Reflect: If your leadership style were a conductor, what would it look like in practice?

How to Use These Talks

To get the most out of these talks, treat them as starting points, not final answers. You might:

  • Watch a talk on your own, then journal two or three insights or questions it raised.
  • Share a link with your team and discuss: “What stood out? What could we apply here?”
  • Use a short clip to open a workshop or training session.
  • Pair a talk with a reflection guide or worksheet for new leaders.

Many of these talks include transcripts and subtitles on the official sites, making them more accessible for different learning preferences and needs.
