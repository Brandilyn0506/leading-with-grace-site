Leading with Grace & Truth

New managers are often pulled between being kind and being clear. This section explores how grace and truth work together rather than compete with each other. Grace shows up as patience, curiosity, and care for the person. Truth shows up as honesty about expectations, priorities, and impact.

  • Show empathy without avoiding hard conversations.
  • Share expectations early so people are not surprised later.
  • Use phrases that balance support and clarity, such as “I care about you and…”.

Building Trust & Respect

Trust is built slowly through small, consistent actions. For many new leaders, the shift from “co-worker” to “manager” can make relationships feel uncertain. This section focuses on predictable habits that help people feel safe working with you.

  • Do what you say you will do, even with small commitments.
  • Explain the “why” behind decisions whenever possible.
  • Give credit publicly and coach privately.
  • Admit mistakes and share what you are learning.

Communicating with Clarity

Clear communication reduces anxiety and rework. For new managers, clarity often means slowing down long enough to check for understanding and invite questions. This section highlights simple structures you can apply to meetings, emails, and coaching conversations.

  • Use plain language and avoid unnecessary jargon.
  • Organize messages around: purpose, key points, and next steps.
  • Ask team members to repeat back key actions in their own words.
  • Follow up in writing after important decisions or commitments.
