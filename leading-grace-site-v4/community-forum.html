Community Forum

A shared space to ask questions, trade ideas, and learn from other leaders practicing grace and truth.

The Community Forum is designed as a supportive environment where leaders can process real situations, share what’s working, and learn from one another’s experience. This isn’t a place for perfect stories; it’s a place for honest questions, practical ideas, and encouragement.

Overview

You’ll find two main spaces within the forum: the Discussion Boards, where you can explore topics with the whole community, and Ask a Mentor, where you can submit specific scenarios for focused guidance. Both are built around the same goal—helping you grow as a leader while honoring the people you lead.

Before posting, take a moment to read through the guidelines below. They’re here to protect the tone of the space and help everyone feel safe contributing.

Participation Guidelines

Community works best when we show up with both grace and truth. Please keep these expectations in mind whenever you post, reply, or react:

  • Protect confidentiality. Remove names, identifying details, and sensitive information.
  • Assume positive intent. Respond as if the other person is trying to learn, not win.
  • Be specific but respectful. Focus on behaviors and situations, not labeling people.
  • Share experience, not prescriptions. Offer “what has helped me” instead of “what you must do.”
  • Honor time. Keep posts focused and actionable so others can follow along easily.
  • Model grace & truth. Be kind and honest at the same time—especially in disagreement.

How to Use the Community Forum

When to use the Discussion Boards

Use the Discussion Boards when you want to:

  • Explore a leadership topic with multiple perspectives.
  • Share a practice, habit, or tool that’s helped your team.
  • Ask, “Has anyone else experienced this?” and gather ideas.
  • Reflect on a recent win or challenge and invite conversation.

When to use Ask a Mentor

Use Ask a Mentor when you:

  • Have a specific situation and want focused, thoughtful input.
  • Need help framing a difficult conversation or decision.
  • Are unsure how to apply grace & truth in a real scenario.
  • Prefer a more guided response instead of open discussion.

In both spaces, you are welcome to bring real questions, unfinished ideas, and “I’m not sure what to do here” moments. That’s where growth usually begins.

Posting Tips

  • Give context, not history. Share just enough background for others to understand the situation.
  • Ask a clear question. Let people know what kind of help you’re hoping for.
  • Circle back. If you receive helpful input, reply with what you tried and what you learned.
  • Encourage others. A simple “thank you, this helped” goes a long way.
