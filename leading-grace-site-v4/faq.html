Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about Leading with Grace & Truth.

This page brings together common questions about how to use Leading with Grace & Truth resources, who the site is for, and what to expect from the tools, videos, and guides provided here. If you don’t see your question answered, you’re always welcome to reach out through the Contact & Support page.

General Questions

These questions cover the purpose of the site, how to use the materials, and what “leading with grace and truth” really looks like in practice.

What is “Leading with Grace & Truth”?

Leading with Grace & Truth is a leadership approach and resource hub focused on blending empathy with clarity. It’s designed to help leaders build strong relationships, communicate honestly, and make decisions that reflect both care for people and commitment to responsibility.

Who is this site intended for?

The site is for leaders and emerging leaders in any setting—workplaces, schools, teams, ministries, or community groups. If you are responsible for guiding others, shaping culture, or influencing decisions, these tools are for you.

How should I use the resources on this site?

You can explore the content in several ways: watch short video tips, reflect using the written guides, bring questions to the Ask a Mentor space, or use discussion prompts with your team. Start with the topic that matches a current situation you’re facing, then apply one small action or question at a time.

Can I share these materials with my team or group?

Yes. You are encouraged to use the concepts, questions, and frameworks with your team, small group, or class. If you’d like help adapting a resource to your setting or building a session around it, you can send a request through the Contact & Support page.

What does it mean to lead with both grace and truth?

Leading with grace means approaching people with empathy, patience, and respect. Leading with truth means being honest, clear, and consistent about expectations and responsibilities. Grace without truth can lead to confusion; truth without grace can lead to disconnection. Strong leadership holds both together.

Are the examples and scenarios based on real situations?

Many of the scenarios are inspired by real leadership experiences, but they are adapted and anonymized to protect confidentiality. The goal is to reflect common patterns leaders face, not to retell specific stories.

Do you offer coaching, workshops, or speaking?

Coaching and workshops may be available depending on timing and fit. If you’re interested in support for your team or organization, use the Contact & Support page to share what you’re looking for, and you’ll receive a follow-up with options and next steps.

What if my question isn’t listed here?

If your question isn’t covered on this page, that’s okay. You can submit it through the Contact & Support page or, if it’s a leadership scenario, through the Ask a Mentor space. Your questions also help shape future resources and FAQs.
