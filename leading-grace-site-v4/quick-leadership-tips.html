Quick Leadership Tips

Simple, actionable tools you can use in under two minutes—right before the moment that matters.

The Quick Leadership Tips section is designed for those times when you don’t have space for a full course or article—you just need a nudge, a phrase, or a structure to help you respond well. Each area below offers short, practical ideas you can apply in real conversations, meetings, and decisions.

Rapid Tips

Use these quick prompts when you’re moving fast but still want to lead with intention. Each one is meant to be read and applied in less than a minute.

Before a 1:1

  • Ask yourself: “What does this person most need from me today—clarity, encouragement, or feedback?”
  • Start with: “What’s on your mind?” and listen fully before adding your agenda.
  • End with: “What are your top priorities between now and our next check-in?”

Before Giving Feedback

  • Shift from blame to partnership: “How can we solve this together?”
  • Use the pattern: When you… (behavior), it impacts… (impact), because… (why it matters).
  • Ask: “What feels realistic for a next step?” instead of prescribing everything yourself.

Before a Tough Conversation

  • Clarify your intention: “My goal is to strengthen our working relationship, not just win a point.”
  • Open with: “I value our work together, and that’s why I want to talk about this.”
  • Plan one sentence that names the issue clearly and kindly—then stop talking and listen.

Checklists & Templates

These simple checklists help you prepare quickly without overcomplicating things. Use them as mental prompts or turn them into printable cards you keep at your desk.

Quick Meeting Checklist

  • Purpose: Do I know why we’re meeting?
  • People: Is everyone here who needs to be—and no one who doesn’t?
  • Plan: Do I have 2–3 key points or decisions to cover?
  • Next Steps: How will we document and share action items?

Change Update Checklist

  • Have I explained why the change is happening?
  • Have I named what is changing—and what is not?
  • Have I shared how this affects this team specifically?
  • Have I given a place/time for questions and follow-up?

1:1 Conversation Template

  • Start: “How are you doing—really?”
  • Work check-in: Wins, current priorities, roadblocks.
  • Support: “What do you need from me?”
  • Future: “Anything you’d like to grow in over the next few months?”
  • Close: Confirm next steps for both of you.

Printable Guides

Printable guides are ideal for posting near your workspace, slipping into a planner, or using as a quick reference before key moments. Below are examples of what can live in this section.

Grace & Truth Reminder Card

  • Grace: Am I seeing the person, not just the problem?
  • Truth: Am I naming the real issue clearly?
  • Next step: What is one thing we can commit to today?

Coaching Question Card

  • “What options have you considered so far?”
  • “What outcome would you most like to see?”
  • “What’s getting in the way—and what’s in your control?”
  • “What’s one step you can take this week?”

Team Check-In Card

  • “What’s going well that we should keep doing?”
  • “What feels heavy or confusing right now?”
  • “Where do you need more clarity or support from me?”
