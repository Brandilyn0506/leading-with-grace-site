Leadership Articles

Curated readings to deepen how you think about leading people, teams, and organizations.

The Leadership Articles page gathers trusted, research-based pieces on topics such as emotional intelligence, manager effectiveness, listening, and human-centered performance. Each article below includes a short summary, why it matters, and a reflection or next step you can apply in your own context.

Overview

These articles are selected to support leaders who want to grow in both character and competence. As you read, consider how each idea connects to your current role, your team, or a specific situation you’re navigating.

What Makes a Leader?

Source: Harvard Business Review · Focus: Emotional Intelligence

Daniel Goleman explains why emotional intelligence matters as much as IQ and technical skill in effective leadership. He highlights five components: self-awareness, self-regulation, motivation, empathy, and social skill.

Why it matters: Leaders who understand and manage themselves are better able to build trust, navigate conflict, and guide others through change without losing connection.

Reflect: Which part of emotional intelligence is a strength for you right now, and which one needs more intentional practice this season?

Engage Your Workforce by Empowering Your Managers First

Source: Gallup · Focus: Manager Effectiveness & Engagement

Gallup shares research showing that engaged managers are the key to engaged teams. The article explores the pressures managers face and how clarity, coaching, and realistic expectations directly influence performance and retention.

Why it matters: Most employees experience the organization through their direct manager. When managers are supported and aligned, the whole team benefits.

Try this: Think of one manager you support. What is one conversation, resource, or boundary that would make their role more sustainable?

Active Listening Techniques: Best Practices for Leaders

Source: Center for Creative Leadership · Focus: Listening & Coaching

The Center for Creative Leadership outlines concrete listening skills—like reflecting, clarifying, and summarizing—that help leaders turn everyday conversations into coaching moments.

Why it matters: Many leadership challenges are really listening challenges. When people feel heard, they are more open to feedback, direction, and change.

Try this: In your next 1:1, aim to listen long enough that you can summarize the other person’s perspective before offering your own.

The Mindsets and Practices of Excellent CEOs

Source: McKinsey & Company · Focus: Senior Leadership & Strategy

McKinsey identifies recurring mindsets and routines among highly effective CEOs, including how they set direction, align the organization, work with the board, and manage their own time and energy.

Why it matters: Even if you’re not a CEO, these patterns provide a helpful picture of accountable, long-horizon leadership.

Reflect: Which mindset described in the article feels most applicable to your level of responsibility right now?

Evolving Leadership to Drive Human Performance

Source: Deloitte · Focus: Human-Centered Leadership

Deloitte explores how leaders can move beyond traditional productivity metrics to focus on “human performance” — creating conditions where people can do meaningful, sustainable work over time.

Why it matters: Lasting performance requires more than tasks and targets. Leaders shape the pace, priorities, and practices that either fuel or drain their teams.

Try this: Choose one initiative or priority and ask, “How does this impact people’s ability to do their best work long-term?” Adjust one expectation accordingly.
