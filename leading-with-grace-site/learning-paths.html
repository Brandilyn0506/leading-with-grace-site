Learning Paths

Guided journeys that build leadership skills one step at a time.

Learning Paths are structured journeys that help leaders grow in focused areas over time. Instead of asking, “Where do I even start?”, each path gives you a clear sequence of topics, resources, and reflection points so you can build confidence and capability in a purposeful way.

Each path is centered on a key aspect of leadership—foundations, coaching, team culture, conflict, engagement, and leading through change—and is designed to be practical for busy managers. You can move through a path on your own, with a mentor, or alongside a small group.

Overview

Learning journeys are designed to be:

  • Modular: Short, focused segments you can complete between meetings or during a week.
  • Layered: Concepts build on each other so you revisit themes with deeper understanding.
  • Practice-based: Each step includes something to try in real conversations or decisions.
  • Reflective: Prompts help you notice what you’re learning, not just what you’re doing.
  • Flexible: You can move in order—or pause and revisit a section when a real need arises.

Over time, Learning Paths can include short videos, checklists, discussion questions, and downloadable tools that support both individual growth and team development.

How Learning Paths Work

  1. Choose a focus: Pick the path that best matches your current season or challenges.
  2. Follow the sequence: Work through modules or sections in order for a full experience.
  3. Apply in real time: Try one idea, question, or practice in your everyday leadership.
  4. Reflect and adjust: Capture what you notice, then refine how you lead the next time.
  5. Revisit as needed: Return to specific sections whenever you face similar situations again.

What Each Path Emphasizes

Each Learning Path has a distinct focus:

  • Leadership Foundations: Core mindsets and practices for new managers—grace & truth, trust, and clear communication.
  • Coaching & Feedback: Everyday conversations that help people grow, stay aligned, and feel supported.
  • Team Culture: The habits, stories, and norms that shape how your team feels and functions.
  • Conflict Resolution: Approaches for handling tension in ways that protect both relationships and results.
  • Motivating & Engaging Teams: Practical ways to keep people energized, recognized, and connected to purpose.
  • Leading Through Change: Guiding others through transitions with clarity, empathy, and stability.
