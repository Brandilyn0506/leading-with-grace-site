Just-In-Time Resources

Short, practical guidance you can use in real-time—right when leadership moments happen.

The Just-In-Time Resources section is designed for those moments when you don’t have time to read a full chapter or attend a training session—you just need a quick reference to help you lead wisely in the next conversation, decision, or challenge. These tools are meant to be brief, actionable, and easy to apply in high-pressure or time-sensitive situations.

Overview

Leaders often describe situations where they thought, “I wish I had the right words right now” or “I need a quick way to reset before I walk into this meeting.” Just-in-time resources exist for exactly those moments. Whether you’re about to give feedback, handle a tough conversation, or encourage someone who’s struggling, these resources help you respond with both clarity and care.

Each resource is designed to help you:

  • Prepare for a leadership moment in 2 minutes or less.
  • Find quick language for conversations (coaching, correction, encouragement).
  • Stay grounded and avoid reacting from stress or frustration.
  • Build confidence in navigating real-time leadership challenges.

When to Use a Just-In-Time Resource

These tools are especially helpful when:

  • You need to respond to a situation unexpectedly.
  • You are preparing for a conversation you’d rather avoid.
  • You’re transitioning from task work to people work (e.g., walking into a 1:1).
  • You want to reinforce a team value in the moment.
  • You’re coaching someone through a roadblock or setback.

Examples of Just-In-Time Tools

  • “Before the Conversation” reset prompts – 30-second mental check-in.
  • Feedback sentence starters – respectful, direct language templates.
  • 1-minute encouragement scripts – when someone needs support quickly.
  • Conflict pause phrases – help slow down emotional escalation.
  • Decision clarity questions – to reframe a choice under pressure.

Try This When You Have 1 Minute

Use one of the following quick resets before reacting or responding:

  • “What does this person need most from me right now?”
  • “What outcome do I want after this conversation?”
  • “How can I say this clearly while still showing care?”
  • “Is my reaction matching the reality, or my stress?”
