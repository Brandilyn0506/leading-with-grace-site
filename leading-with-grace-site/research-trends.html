Research & Trends

Current findings, big-picture trends, and deeper dives for leaders who want to stay informed and intentional.

The Research & Trends page highlights studies, reports, and perspectives that shape how we think about work today—engagement, culture, hybrid work, well-being, and leadership expectations. The goal isn’t to drown you in data, but to offer a few carefully selected pieces you can actually use.

Overview

Each resource below includes a brief summary, why it matters, and a reflection or question to bring back to your own team. You can use these insights to sharpen strategy, support your people, or simply frame better conversations about how you work together.

Current Findings

Global Engagement & Well-Being

Source: Gallup – State of the Global Workplace

Gallup’s ongoing research tracks how people feel about their work, their stress levels, and whether they feel their opinions count. The reports highlight gaps between engaged and disengaged employees and the critical role of the manager.

Why it matters: Engagement is not just a “nice-to-have.” It’s tied to performance, retention, and how people talk about your organization outside the building.

Reflect: If you asked your team how they’re doing right now, what do you think they would say— and how do you know?

View Gallup Report

Trend Spotlight

The Evolving Workforce & Human Sustainability

Source: Deloitte – Global Human Capital Trends

Deloitte’s research explores how organizations are rethinking work, skills, and leadership—moving from simple productivity metrics toward “human sustainability” and long-term capability building.

Why it matters: Leaders are expected to deliver results and protect the people who deliver them. Ignoring well-being and development has real costs over time.

Try this: Choose one policy, process, or habit and ask: “Does this help or hinder people’s ability to do great work over the long term?”

Explore Deloitte Trends

Future of Work

How Work Is Changing (and What Leaders Can Do)

Source: McKinsey & Company – Future of Work & Organizations

McKinsey’s organizational research highlights shifts in technology, hybrid work, skills, and leadership models. It emphasizes adaptability, clarity of purpose, and the need for leaders who can make decisions amid ambiguity.

Why it matters: The context you’re leading in is changing whether you plan for it or not. Understanding the larger landscape helps you make grounded, forward-looking decisions.

Reflect: Where is your team already adapting well, and where are you still using “old rules” for a new reality?

View McKinsey Insights

Trust & Reputation

Trust in Leaders & Institutions

Source: Edelman – Trust Barometer

Edelman’s annual Trust Barometer examines how much people trust business, government, media, and NGOs—and where leaders are expected to step in on social and workplace issues. Business often emerges as the most trusted institution, raising the bar on leaders’ responsibilities.

Why it matters: Trust is a leadership asset you build slowly and lose quickly. Understanding what builds (or erodes) trust helps you lead with steadiness and credibility.

Try this: Ask yourself: “If someone looked only at my actions over the last month, would they see reasons to trust my words?”

Explore Edelman Findings

Recommended Reading

These books and reports offer deeper dives for leaders who want to connect day-to-day decisions with the bigger picture of where work is headed.

Books

  • Leaders Eat Last – Simon Sinek
    Explores how trust, safety, and service shape healthy, high-performing teams.
  • Dare to Lead – Brené Brown
    Focuses on courage, vulnerability, and the practical skills behind brave leadership.
  • The Advantage – Patrick Lencioni
    Makes the case that organizational health is a leader’s greatest strategic edge.

Articles & Reports

  • HBR – “The C-Suite Skills That Matter Most”
    Summarizes which leadership skills are rising in importance at the top levels.
  • Gallup – State of the American Workplace
    Provides detailed data on engagement, expectations, and manager impact.
  • World Economic Forum – Future of Jobs Report
    Looks ahead at skills, roles, and trends shaping the future labor market.

Use these resources as conversation starters, not prescriptions. The most important question is always: “What does this mean for the people I’m leading right now?”
